Paul Poiret Catwalk History Appropriation

Paul Poiret

"The most enterprising new Parisian couturier of the day, Paul Poiret, introduced himself to high society and all of the crowned heads of Europe in his self-promoting catalogue, Les robes de Paul Poiret. Illustrated by the young artist Paul Iribe in original, highly colorful stencil prints, this first landmark album of Art Deco fashion plates began a new, far more liberalized image of women through high fashion."

Exoticism - Schéhérazade in Paris

"The exotic influence of Les Ballets Russes and its Schéhérazade was phenomenal; it turned the fashion world of Paris into an extravagant Persian bazaar. The energetic couturier Paul Poiret, already high arbiter of women’s dress design, had developed his own passion for the magic of the Arabian Nights and introduced harem skirts, silk pants, and—as a Poiret signature touch—gold lamé turbans flaunting egret plumes."

Dale Roylance, "Art Deco Paris 1900-1925: Catalogue of the Exhibitino of Pochoir Color Prints from the Graphic Arts Collection, in: The Princeton University Library Chronicle, 61.1, 1999, 1-72."
