Before getting started with the exact or even the vague structure, my project partner and I both decided to do some research on our own. The main goal of this being that we would both get a general overview of the topic and ideally would already find topics we would like to do further research on.
After we had done our own research, we arranged to establish a first draft for a structure (table above), so that each one of us could now start to research more focused.
One reoccurring topic which presented itself, was the importance of the Sakoku, respectively the closure of Japan and the large interest that was sparked on a global scale when in 1868 Japan loosened its international trade regulations.
Although many of the texts mention how Japanese – or supposedly Japanese – goods came to Europe and America, Daniel D. Hill also highlights in “Chapter 10 – Japan” of History of World Costume and Fashion how Chinese immigrants had a big impact on the techniques as well as designs of Japanese textile production.
Although generally not labelled as Japonisme today, there are still multiple examples that show people’s fascination for Japanese aesthetics and artefacts, not seldom – Kimonos, remain to this day.
Many different aspects of Japanese Culture were emulated, appropriated, and in some cases translated into Western Culture. Around the turn of the century (19th/20th), in Western fashion, one of the major impacts Japanese fashion seems to have had, was the "loosening" of the silhouette.
